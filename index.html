Jeff Bezos

Jeff Bezos, born on January 12, 1964, in Albuquerque, New Mexico, is the founder of Amazon.com, which he launched in 1995 as an online bookstore that rapidly evolved into a global e-commerce giant. A Princeton graduate in electrical engineering and computer science, Bezos worked on Wall Street before starting Amazon. He also founded Blue Origin, a space exploration company, and purchased The Washington Post in 2013. After stepping down as Amazon's CEO in 2021, Bezos has focused on philanthropy and his other ventures, maintaining his status as one of the world's wealthiest individuals.
