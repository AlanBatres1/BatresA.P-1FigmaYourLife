Bezos launched Amazon in 1995, transforming it from an online bookstore into one of the world’s largest e-commerce platforms, revolutionizing retail.

He oversaw the development of AWS, which has become a leading provider of cloud computing services, contributing significantly to Amazon's profitability.

In 2013, Bezos purchased The Washington Post for $250 million, revitalizing its digital strategy and expanding its reach.

He founded Blue Origin in 2000, aiming to advance space travel and exploration, with successful suborbital flights and plans for future missions.

Through the Bezos Day One Fund and the Bezos Earth Fund, he has committed substantial resources to addressing issues like homelessness and climate change.

Bezos has consistently ranked among the world’s wealthiest individuals, with his net worth peaking significantly due to Amazon's stock performance.

His innovations and leadership style have influenced the tech industry and entrepreneurial practices, emphasizing customer focus and long-term thinking.

Under his leadership, Amazon expanded into various sectors, including streaming (Amazon Prime Video), smart devices (Alexa), and grocery (Whole Foods acquisition). \
